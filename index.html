How to use
ResumeTalk

Follow these steps. No tech skills needed — if you can talk, you can build a great resume.

1

Choose your template

Pick from 25 professionally designed resume styles. Classic for most jobs, Bold for creative roles, Modern for tech.

Tip: You can change your template after your resume is built — just start over.
2

Solo or interview mode

Speak alone, or have a job developer ask you questions while you answer. ResumeTalk auto-detects both modes.

Solo mode
One person speaks their full story.
Interview mode
Job developer asks, candidate answers. AI extracts candidate info only.
Tip: Use Chrome or Edge for the best microphone experience.
3

Exactly what to say

Start with your personal details, then each job, then education, then what you're looking for.

① Personal info
"My name is James Williams. My number is 917-555-0123. I live in Brooklyn, New York."
② Each job
"I worked at FedEx in Brooklyn from January 2017 to June 2022. I was a package handler. I sorted packages and trained new staff."
⚠ Say the month AND year — "January 2017" not just "2017"
③ Education
"I went to Brooklyn Tech, graduated 2013. I got a forklift certificate in 2018."
④ What you want
"I'm looking for a warehouse supervisor role in New York."
Don't worry about being perfect. Speak naturally — ResumeTalk understands everyday speech.
4

Watch your resume come to life

Your words float up on screen as the AI reads your story and builds your resume in real time.

5

Download and start applying

Get your styled resume, 15+ job recommendations with salary ranges, industry fit scores, and transferable skills. Download as Word or plain text.

Next step: Open in Google Docs or Word to make final edits before sending to employers.
