ResumeTalk works two ways. You can speak alone, or a job developer can guide the conversation by asking questions while the candidate answers. ResumeTalk automatically detects which mode you're in and extracts the right information either way.

3

Exactly what to say — and how to say it

Start with your personal details, then walk through each job you've had. Speak in full sentences — the more detail you give, the better your resume will be. Here's the exact order to follow:

① Start with your personal info
Say your full name, phone number, and where you live.
"My name is James Williams. My phone number is 917-555-0123. I live in Brooklyn, New York."
② For each job — say the company, location, and exact dates
Say the name of the company, where it was, and the month and year you started and left. Then describe what you did there.
"I worked at FedEx in Brooklyn, New York from January 2017 to June 2022. I was a package handler. I sorted and loaded packages, made sure deliveries went to the right places, and trained new staff members."
⚠ Watch your dates — say the month AND the year clearly, for example "January 2017" not just "2017".
③ Repeat for every job you've had
Go through every job, part-time work, or volunteer position. Most recent job first.
"Before that, I worked at McDonald's in Flatbush, Brooklyn from June 2014 to December 2016. I was a crew member. I prepared food, handled the cash register, and helped keep the restaurant clean during busy shifts."
④ Talk about your education and training
Mention any school, certificate, course, or training program — even if you didn't finish.
"I went to Brooklyn Technical High School and graduated in 2013. I also completed a forklift certification in 2018."
⑤ Finish with your skills and what you're looking for
Tell us what you're good at and what kind of job you want next.
"I'm good at working with people, staying organized under pressure, and learning new things fast. I'm looking for a warehouse supervisor or logistics coordinator role in the New York area."
Don't worry about being perfect. Speak naturally — even if you stumble or repeat yourself. ResumeTalk understands everyday speech and will turn it into clean, professional resume language. You can also edit your transcript before building.

Common questions
Is ResumeTalk really free?+
Yes, completely free. ResumeTalk is a nonprofit tool. There is no subscription, no hidden fee, and no account required.
Is my information private?+
Yes. Your voice and transcript are never stored on any server. Everything is processed within your browser session and is gone when you close the tab.
What if I don't have much work experience?+
Talk about volunteer work, community involvement, caring for family, school projects, or any skills you have. The AI will find the value in everything you share.
Can I use it on my phone?+
Yes. ResumeTalk works on smartphones and tablets. Use Chrome on Android or Safari on iPhone for the best microphone experience.
